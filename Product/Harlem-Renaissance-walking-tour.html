Harlem Renaissance Walking Tour

Historian-led walking tour through the heart of Harlem’s cultural, political, and musical history.

Tour Overview

This 3-hour walking tour explores the Harlem Renaissance through the lens of a licensed New York City historian. You’ll walk the streets where writers, musicians, activists, and community leaders reshaped American culture, and see how Harlem’s past connects directly to its present.

Key Stops on the Tour

  • Apollo Theater – Iconic performance venue and symbol of Black entertainment history.
  • Savoy Ballroom site – Former home of one of Harlem’s most famous swing dance halls.
  • Renaissance Ballroom – A cornerstone of Harlem’s social and cultural life.
  • Schomburg Center for Research in Black Culture – Archive and research center preserving Black history.
  • Hotel Theresa – “The Waldorf of Harlem,” central to mid-20th century Black politics and culture.
  • Abyssinian Baptist Church – Spiritual and political anchor of Harlem’s Black community.
  • Striver’s Row – Historic row houses tied to Harlem’s Black professional class.
  • Billie Holiday’s birth house – 133rd Street between Lenox Avenue and 7th Avenue, connecting Harlem’s entertainment district to the evolution of jazz.
  • Modern Harlem churches & gentrification – How major churches helped shape Harlem’s redevelopment over the last 25 years.

Harlem Then & Now

This tour doesn’t just look back at the Harlem Renaissance—it shows how its legacy lives on today. From historic theaters and ballrooms to churches that became major players in Harlem’s redevelopment, you’ll see how culture, politics, faith, and real estate all intersect in one neighborhood.
