Gangs of New York – 5-Hour Walking Tour

From Battery Park to Hell’s Kitchen — the complete criminal, political, and immigrant history of Manhattan.

Tour Overview

This 5-hour historian-led walking tour traces the true story behind Gangs of New York and the evolution of Manhattan’s underworld. From the earliest colonial settlements to the Five Points gangs, Tammany Hall, Prohibition, the Mafia, and the rise of the Westies, this tour follows the full arc of New York’s criminal and political history. The route begins at Battery Park and ends in Hell’s Kitchen, covering the neighborhoods, families, and power structures that shaped the city.

Route & Key Stops

  • Battery Park / National Museum of the American Indian – Dutch, British, and early American power; immigration before Ellis Island.
  • Wall Street & Trinity Church – The original wall of New Amsterdam, Hamilton’s grave, early policing, and financial corruption.
  • Five Points – The heart of New York’s gang history: Dead Rabbits, Bowery Boys, Draft Riots, and the transformation of the neighborhood into the modern city, state, and federal court system.
  • Collect Pond & The Tombs – From polluted pond to notorious prison; the roots of New York’s criminal justice system.
  • Mulberry Bend & Chinatown – Jacob Riis, tenement reform, tongs, and the shift from Irish and Italian gangs to Chinese organized crime.
  • Little Italy – The rise of the Morello family, the Genovese family, Prohibition networks, and the consolidation of the Italian Mafia.
  • Union Square – Birthplace of the Democratic political machine; Boss Tweed, labor organizing, and immigrant political power.
  • Midtown Manhattan – Rockefeller’s takeover of the underground social-club district; Prohibition speakeasies; the shift from Old Broadway (below 14th Street) to New Broadway (Times Square); the wholesale district; the original Madison Square Garden; and the development of the Fashion District.
  • East & West Side Projects – Post-WWII urban renewal, displacement, and the reshaping of Manhattan’s working-class neighborhoods.
  • Hell’s Kitchen – Final stop. Home of the Westies, Irish mob dominance, labor racketeering, and the last major chapter of Manhattan’s gangland history.

What You’ll Learn

This tour connects the entire criminal and political evolution of Manhattan: immigration waves, class warfare, the Draft Riots, Tammany Hall, Prohibition, Mafia families, Jewish organized crime, the rise of the Westies, and the real-estate power shifts that reshaped the city. You’ll see how the same streets that once held the world’s most dangerous slums now hold courthouses, corporate towers, and some of the most valuable real estate in the world.
